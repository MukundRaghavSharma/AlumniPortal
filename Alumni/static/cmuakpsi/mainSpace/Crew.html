About

Tartan Crew is a competitive rowing team that allows student-athletes the opportunity to represent Carnegie Mellon University at regattas throughout the east coast, competing against hundreds of other collegiate rowing programs. The team races in the fall and spring, while spending the winter season training indoors. Despite having only been established in 1987, the team has proved to be a true contender at large regattas such as the Dad Vails National Championship and the Head of the Ohio (hosted every October right in Pittsburgh).

The team mainly competes with schools within the University Rowing Association (URA), which contains all the schools in Carnegie Mellon's official athletic conference. These include Brandeis University, Carnegie Mellon University, Case Western Reserve University, Emory University, New York University, University of Chicago, University of Rochester, and Washington University in St. Louis. However, as the team attends various regattas, other frequent competitors include University of Pittsburgh, Duquesne, Fordham, Lehigh, Penn State, and Duke. We cross hundreds more at large regattas.

Applying:
Many of our rowers join with little to no experience in the sport, but with strong backgrounds in a variety of other sports. For them, rowing provides a new physically demanding environment in which to explore, and eventually, excel. Also, some rowers join the team with no athletic experience, and find rowing to be that physically challenging and rewarding activity they had long been looking for. Interested in joining? Want to learn more? Contact Julia Embody at publicity@tartancrew.org.
