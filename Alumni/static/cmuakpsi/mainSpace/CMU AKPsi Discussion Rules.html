Overview

  • For voting, each candidate will be discussed individually and then the candidates will be compared. Moving to open is not necessary
  • A brother may move to close whenever they feel a candidate has been fully discussed
    • A brother may object to closing, and if they have a "second" then discussion will resume with new information until there is another move to close
  • Each brother may only speak once per discussion, unless they're responding to a request for information
    • A discussion is anything leading up to a "move to close." So in the event of an election between two people, you could speak once during the discussion of the first candidate, once during the discussion of the second, and once during the comparison
  • A queue system will be used to keep track of who should be speaking next
    • This queue is managed by the Secretary and is projected behind the MOR while he or she is running discussion
  • A sub-queue will be used for tracking responses to requests for information
    • Brothers may join the sub-queue by raising two fingers instead of their whole hand
  • Brothers are not required to lead the discussion with a "pro-con-pro," they may instead share any information they please
    • If discussion does not immediately start, the MOR can ask for leading questions such as "Does anyone have a pro?"
  • Brothers who have to be "hushed" more than once will be relocated to a different part of the room
    • Further hushing will result in removal from the deliberation, and the associated fines
