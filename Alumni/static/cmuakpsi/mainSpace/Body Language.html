The Face

Positive

  • Eyebrow raising when greeting someone shows happiness
  • Head tilt is usually a sign of comfort, it exposes the neck
  • Arched eyebrows when expressing a point implies confidence
  • Shaking the head in agreement with that they're saying

Negative

  • Eye blocking, either from squinting or physically obstructing the eyes
    • Implies that they are seeing something undesirable
  • Pulling their lips together so they seem to disappear
    • Implies that they are troubled and something is wrong, they may have something to say that they don't think they should or wont
  • Neck/lips/cheeks/general facial touching or stroking
    • Pacifying behavior that implies that someone is uncomfortable
  • Looking away or lowering eyebrows implies a low confidence in what's being said
  • Sudden eyelid flutters imply a subject has become controversial
  • When someone shakes their head counter to what they're saying, or after a delay from saying it, it implies deception

The Arms

Positive

  • Moving freely and emotively, anything defying gravity
  • Hands on hips implies dominance, as does the arms spread behind the head

Negative

  • Succumbing to gravity, falling by ones side
  • Withdrawing arms
  • Arms behind the back
    • Implies that you don't want someone to get close and you don't want to touch them


The Torso

Positive

  • Leaning towards someone
    • Implies interest and belief
  • Torso bow

Negative

  • Leaning away from someone
    • Implies that you want to be away from them
  • Turtle effect, pulling shoulders up to ears
    • Demonstrates that they are trying to hide
  • Slowly turning the torso away from people we dislike
  • Ventral denial, blocking off of the chest either with objects or body parts
  • Splaying -- taking up as much territory as possible with our bodies
    • Sign of overconfidence, or insolence, or disrespect to superiors
  • Half shrugs -- usually a sign of not being committed to the answer and imply deceit



The Hands

Positive

  • Hand steepling -- putting your hands together with your fingers out and pressed against each other is a very high confidence tell
  • Thumb sticking out of pocket -- very high confidence
  • Fingers interlaced with the thumbs up -- high confidence

Negative

  • Self-preening during a conversation implies disinterest
  • Finger interlocking with thumbs down shows a lack of confidence or certainty
  • Thumbs in pockets with fingers out -- low confidence


The Legs

Positive

  • Happy feet-- when your legs or feet bounce up and down in excitement
    • Can be detected by looking at people's shoulders for the subtle vibrations it causes
  • Leg crossing can be a sign of confidence around those whom we trust
    • It is also a positive sign when the feet that are crossed are pointed towards the person
    • It is negative if the feet are pointed towards the door
  • Isopraxis -- mirroring other peoples' leg behavior

Negative

  • Pointing feet away from you or towards the exit while talking to you
    • Implies they want to leave or talk to someone else
  • Spreading of the feet while talking
    • Their legs spreading implies they're getting uncomfortable
  • Crossing the legs so that the leg blocks the hips from someone else, switching legs to do this suddenly
    • Implies you want to protect yourself from them
  • Suddenly turning toes inward or interlocking of feet
    • Sign of being threatened or insecure, anxious
  • Pulling feet under the chair
    • Sign of wanting to escape, be as far away as possible
