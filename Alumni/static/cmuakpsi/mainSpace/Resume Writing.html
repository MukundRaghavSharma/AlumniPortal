Background

This is meant to be a comprehensive guide to writing a good resume

What Recruiters Look For

  • Familiar Patterns
  • Relevant Predictors of Success and Fit
  • Impressive and Unique Accomplishments
  • Glaring Errors
  • People with Good Change of Interview Success and Job Acceptance

Most Important Information

Name and Contact Info

  • Name
  • Cell Phone
  • Email (.edu)
  • Clear Placement

Education

  • Institutions
    • Name and Location
    • Prominent HS if applicable
  • Degree
    • Major(s), Minor(s), Concentration(s)
    • SAT/Relevant Test Scores
  • Cumulative GPA
    • Out of 4.00
    • Add In-Major GPA if helpful
  • Coursework
    • Only what's relevant
    • Only if you're ready to be quizzed on it
  • Qualifications/Certifications/Honors
    • If relevant and legitimate

Experience

  • Work Experience
    • Internships/Co-Ops
    • Relevant Jobs
    • Research work
  • Project Experience
    • Client project
    • Class projects
    • Start-up/Entrepreneurial work
    • Relevant only
  • Be sure to include:
    • Location
    • Job title and Group title
    • Ability to work with diverse people and situations
    • Results, metrics, specific impact, Numbers

Leadership and Activities

  • Leadership
    • Position in Organization
    • Founding roles
    • Impact of Leadership
  • Activities
    • Athletics and Student Groups
  • Relevant if it demonstrates:
    • Diversity, Flexibility, Growth
    • Working in teams
    • Time management
    • Leadership
    • Impact on organization, school, and students

  • Realize the most important skills and abilities for your industry of interest, and cater to them

Skills

  • Relevant Only
    • Languages
    • Programming Languages
    • Software use
      • Microsoft Office, AutoCAD, etc
  • Less important for Non-technical majors
  • Do not embellish at all

Something to Show You're Human

  • Sports
  • Activities
  • A few things you could potentially have in common with a recruiter that's worth talking about
  • Not more than 1 line

Structure

  • Rules
    • One Page
    • No Objectives/References
  • Justifying Variation
    • To demonstrate creativity
    • If interested in design work
    • Manipulating whitespace
    • Fitting industry standards
    • Showcasing certain sections
  • Things to vary
    • Font
    • Bold/Italics
    • Size
    • White Space
    • Chronological vs Importance
    • Method of displaying date
    • Header layout

Aesthetics

  • Fonts
    • Serif for slowing reading, Sans for faster reading
    • No smaller than 10pt
    • Professional
  • Graphical Objects
    • No Objects
    • Can choose square vs circle bullets
    • No lines down the sides, can be appropriate between sections
  • White Space
    • Use to make the resume more appealing
    • Not too much, not too little

Bullet Crafting

  • Readdress each bullet multiple times
    • Make sure structure is consistent throughout resume
      • Beginning, middle, end, impact, etc
    • Always start with action verbs (unless inappropriate)
    • Simplified grammar
    • Don't overuse words or phrases, especially for starting or ending
  • Each bullet should show:
    • Significant roles and responsibilities
    • Quantifiable impact and results
    • Leadership with numbers
    • Impressive achievements
