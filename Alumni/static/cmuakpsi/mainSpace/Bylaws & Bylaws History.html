Mission Statement

To develop well-trained, ethical, skilled, resourceful, experienced business leaders.


Vision Statement

Alpha Kappa Psi is recognized as the premier developer of principled business leaders.


Rules of Thumb

  • The e-board will look into reasons behind any brother missing more than 3 mandatory events/meetings, regardless of whether or not they were excused.
  • Mandatory events refer only to general body meetings and fraternity rituals. However, attending events that aren’t necessarily mandatory is still vital to the survival of the chapter as well as the integrity of a brother.


Membership

  • Good standing – All brothers begin in good standing and remain in good standing as long as their accounts remain balanced and attend all required events/meetings.
  • Bad Standing (suspension) – A brother automatically go into bad standing and are suspended if he/she misses 3 mandatory events/meetings without a valid excuse. A brother may also be in bad standing if they refuse to pay any of their financial obligations within 30 days of the due date. If a brother remains in bad standing for 30 days, he/she is up for expulsion.
  • Inactive – A brother may opt to become inactive for one semester during their time at a chapter. A brother must inform the E-board the semester prior to the inactive one. Inactive brothers are exempt from all financial obligations but have no voting privileges during their duration of their inactivity.
  • Suspension/Expulsion – Brothers that have been suspended have the chance to automatically return to good standing by providing a valid reason for being absent and/or fulfilling all financial obligations. A brother that is up for expulsion has a chance to present their case to the E-board before being expelled. An expelled brother may petition the Regional Director (Yakov Shenkman) for reinstatement back into the fraternity.


Officers (Operations, Requirements, Length in Office)

  • Executives
  • Committee Chairs


Voting

  • Quorum Defined – Quorum constitutes the majority of brothers in good standing at a meeting (50%+1).
  • Voting Methods – For rushees and all pledging rituals, we use a secret ballot. For a rushee or a pledge to be admitted, more than 75% of brothers did not abstain must vote yes (conversely: less than 25% of brothers who did not abstain must vote no).
  • Elections – For elections, we do a heads down, hands up vote. Quorum defines the winner. If there are more than 2 candidates, there is a run off.


Amendments to Bylaws

  • Procedure – Any brother may present a change for bylaws. Proposals must be brought up a week prior to actual consideration of the amendment.
  • The only way to waive the week delay is if all brothers in good standing vote unanimously in favor of the proposal.


Inactive Brothers

  • Any brother in good standing is eligible to declare one's self inactive for 1 semester during their studies at Carnegie Mellon University.
  • The brother is obligated to inform the Executive board in writing with the reasons for deciding to being inactive, in the semester preceding the inactive semester.
  • An inactive brother who is inactive for any reason other than absence from campus (ie, study abroad, serious illness) will still be responsible for paying full dues including national plus the chapter's dues.
  • Inactive brothers who are inactive due to legitimate reasons as sanctioned by nationals will be exempt from such dues.
  • An inactive Brother is welcome to all events excluding rituals.The inactive Brother will not have any voting rights, will not be able to be a 'Big', nor be permitted to live in SIH.
  • An inactive brother may not re-activate themselves during the semester they have elected to be inactive. A brother may still be active in many aspects of the fraternity (except for the above stipulations).
