About

Mission - Yesterday, today, and tomorrow, Kappa Alpha Theta exists to nurture each member throughout her college and alumnae experience and to offer a lifelong opportunity for social, intellectual, and moral growth as she meets the higher and broader demands of mature life.

The intellectual ambition of the Fraternity shall be the attainment of highest scholarship. The social aim of the Fraternity shall be to exercise the widest influence for good. The moral aim of the Fraternity shall be the standard of love.

Overview- Kappa Alpha Theta was founded in 1870 as the first Greek-letter fraternity for women. In 1944 the Gamma Theta chapter was installed at Carnegie Mellon University. Kappa Alpha Theta supports Court Appointed Special Advocates (CASA) and the Kappa Alpha Theta Foundation. We hold various fundraising events through the year including Mr. University, Kicks for CASA, Zumba for CASA, and CASA Coffeehouse.
