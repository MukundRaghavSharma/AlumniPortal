Class Overview

Professor: Robert Cavalier
Difficulty: A if you do the work
Hours/Week: 3hr/week for class (mandatory), 1-2hr if you do the readings, 0 if you don't
Homework: Readings only, nothing to turn in
Exams: 2 take home exams, 2 in class exams, one final paper

Major Topics

  • History of Ethics
    • The Life of Socrates
    • Greek Moral Philosophy
    • Hellenistic and Roman Ethics
    • Early Christian Ethics
    • Modern Moral Philosophy
    • Recent Moral Philosophy
  • Concepts and Problems of Ethics
    • Meta-ethics, Normative Ethics, and Applied Ethics
    • Ethical Relativism
    • Ethical Egoism
    • Utilitarian Theories
    • Deontological Ethics
    • Contractarian Theories
    • Virtue Ethics
    • Liberal Rights and Communitarian Theories
    • Case-Based Moral Reasoning and Casuistry
    • Moral Pluralism
  • Applied Ethics
    • The Field of Applied Ethics
    • The Topic of Euthanasia
    • The Topic of Abortion
    • Conflict Resolution

Exams/Papers

Exam 1

The first exam was a take-home series of essays on the topics ranging from Socrates to Early Christian Ethics. Each essay was only 300-500 words, and you only had to write three of them.

Exam 2

The second exam was an in-class series of essays on Early Christian Ethics, Modern Moral Philosophy, and Recent Moral Philosophy.

Exam 3

The third exam was a take-home series of essays on Meta-ethics, Normative Ethics, Applied Ethics, Relativism, Egoism, and Utilitarianism. It involved discussions of what the philosophies entailed, as well as a case analysis employing multiple theories. You again only had to write 3 300-500 word essays.

Exam 4

The fourth exam was an in-class series of essays looking at cases from the perspectives of Utilitarianism, Virtue Ethics, Casuistry, and Care Ethics.
