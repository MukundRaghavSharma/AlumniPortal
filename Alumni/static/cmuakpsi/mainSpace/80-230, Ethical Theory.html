Class Overview

Professor: David Gray, or Alex London
Difficulty: Moderate, you'll have to try to get an A
Hours/Week: 1-2 hours of reading (if you do them carefully/sufficiently for the class discussion)
Homework: No homework aside from readings. There are reading questions that accompany them, but these aren't handed in and are more to guide what you look for when you read.
Exams: Six papers. 3 are less than 1,000 words, 3 are about 2,000

Major Topics

  • Concepts in Normative Ethics (Shelly Kagan)
    • Factors vs. Foundations
    • Well Being
    • Comprehensive vs. Culminative
    • Hedonism
    • Goodness of Outcomes
    • Ethical Options
    • Ethical Rights
    • Normative Foundations
  • Jeremy Bentham
    • Factoral Utilitarianism
    • Quantitative Hedonism
    • Utilitarian Calculus
  • John Stuart Mill (Utilitarianism, On Liberty)
    • Qualitative Hedonism (vs. Bentham's Quantitative)
    • Eudaimonism
    • Instrumental vs. Intrinsic value
    • Tyranny of the Majority
    • The Harm Principle
    • Freedom of Expression and Consciousness
    • Individuality
  • Immanuel Kant (Groundwork on the Metaphysics of Morals)
    • Strict Deontology
    • The Good Will
    • Maxims, Duty
    • The Categorical Imperative (and its three forms)
    • The problem of Happiness
    • The Formula of Humanity
    • The Kingdom of Ends
  • Aristotle
    • Happiness
    • Eudaimonism
    • Distinction of Humanity
    • Reason and Rationality
    • Doctrine of the Mean
    • Essentialism
    • Tri-partite Soul

Exams/Papers

  • Analytic Summary #1
    • Summarize how Kagan discusses the problem of "Well Being" in Normative Ethics
  • Analytic Summary #2
    • What is attractive and unattractive about Options in ethical decision making according to Kagan?
  • Position Paper #1
    • If you are committed to Egoism at the level of Foundations, are you also committed to Egoism at the level of Factors?
  • Analytic Essay #1
    • Is John Stuart Mill a Foundational Egoist or a Foundational Consequentialist?
  • Analytic Essay #2
    • What is Kant's one end for humanity, and how does he justify it?
  • Analytic Essay #3
    • What does Aristotle support at the level of Foundations? Teleology? Deontology? And if Teleology then what teleology?
