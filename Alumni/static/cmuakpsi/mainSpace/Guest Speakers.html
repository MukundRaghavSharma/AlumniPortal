Aimee Schmidt, Fall 2009

Position: Founder of Solutions Consulting, owner of independent consulting company and co-owner of Creative Dinners, CMU graduate
Contact info: aimee@creativedinnersandmore.com

Jason Glance, Fall 2009

Position: Developer at Songwhale
Contact info: jasong@songwhale.com
Topic: Jason gave us a presentation about how Songwhale works and how they developed a business plan. It is a digital content delivery service that teams up with big companies (Steelers, Pirates, Gatorade, Nike, Pizza Hut etc) to offer promotions that aim to make events more interactive. He set up a demonstration promotion for our event. Each brother in attendance texted CMU to WHALE and received a message in return. Two brothers won prizes just as someone might win in a real promotion. Songwhale was started only a few years ago and has been expanding rapidly ever since. The entire staff is pretty young and really enthusiastic about what they do.

R.F. Culbertson, Fall 2009

Position: Professor at CMU for Intro to Entrepreneurship and founder of Get ABBY, CMU graduate
Contact Info: rfc@getabby.com
Topic: R.F. gave one of the lectures that he gave to his Intro to Entrepreneurship that dealt with sales, marketing and being persuasive. Michael Kobold, Fall 2009

Position: President and founder of Kobold watches, CMU graduate
Contact info: mkobold@koboldwatch.com
Topic: Michael started his company as part of a school project at CMU and it has recently been named as one of the ten best watch companies in the world. He spoke to us about what it was like starting a company from scratch and on your own and every aspect that you have to develop. He took us through his marketing campaigns, financial plans and sales strategies. He talked about his journey climbing Mount Everest and training with the Navy Seals in order to promote the image that his watches are intended to give. He was a really entertaining speaker because he graduated fairly recently and can relate to students here very well.

Nick Follini, Spring 2009

Contacts: Nick Frollini: nfrollini@cmu.edu
Event Recap: Alpha Kappa Psi invited Nick Frollini, the Associate Director of the Institute for Software Research in the School of Computer Science at Carnegie Mellon University, to share his expertise on effective presentation skills. He gave a very informative presentation on the few keys to building and delivering a good presentation. He had a lot of insight to offer due to his extensive background in a number of industries including banking, entrepreneurship, and technology. There was pizza and drinks.
